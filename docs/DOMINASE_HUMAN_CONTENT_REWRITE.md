# DOMINASE Human Content Rewrite Proposal

Documentation-only content strategy proposal. This pass does not rewrite website source files, change UI, alter components, update metadata, or modify content configuration.

## 1. Current Content Diagnosis

The current DOMINASE content has a strong visual and strategic foundation, but the language often stays too abstract. Phrases like "Digital systems built to move business," "trust, clarity, and action," and "one connected system" communicate ambition, but they do not always show the practical situation a business owner recognizes: a customer comparing options, an unclear booking path, a weak first impression, or a team repeating the same follow-up manually.

Several words carry too much weight because they repeat across pages: "cinematic," "system," "trust," "clarity," "action," "premium," and "polished." These words are useful, but overuse makes the brand feel more like a category than a point of view. The rewrite should keep the calm premium tone while using more concrete business language.

The site needs more human pain. The strongest direction is already present on `/why-change`, but it should become more specific: customers compare before they call, unclear pages create hesitation, outdated websites make strong businesses look smaller, manual follow-up slows decisions, and trust is affected before the first conversation.

Some claims need safer handling. Named testimonials, broad transformation claims, real project claims, performance promises, and business outcome language should be verified, softened, or tied to visible evidence. The rewrite should not invent metrics, client approvals, conversion results, or testimonials.

The case studies have useful before/after material, but they need more client context, constraints, visible proof, and honest outcome framing. They should show what was messy before, what DOMINASE built, what became easier, what is proven by screenshots, and what is an inferred qualitative improvement.

Arabic coverage exists across the main content systems, but it needs a serious normalization pass. Some output in the audit indicates mojibake or stiff literal phrasing in places. Arabic should read as a parallel brand experience, not as a translation afterthought.

The audit found many hardcoded strings. Some are technical false positives, but visible labels, aria labels, preloader text, form text, project controls, and bilingual microcopy should later move into a content configuration for consistency.

## 2. New DOMINASE Voice

DOMINASE should sound human, sharp, premium, calm, business-aware, credible, and bilingual.

The new voice should feel like a focused digital product studio speaking to a serious business owner, founder, director, or operator. It should explain what DOMINASE builds, why it matters, and what changes for the business without pretending to be a huge agency or making unsupported claims.

DOMINASE remains the main brand. Mohammed Aldomi appears as the founder and leader only when founder credibility helps the reader understand the care, judgment, or accountability behind the work.

Write with:

- practical business context
- clear before/after situations
- concrete examples from websites, booking flows, dashboards, forms, and operational interfaces
- restrained confidence
- honest claims
- Arabic and English that feel equally intentional

Avoid:

- fake luxury language
- inflated agency slogans
- robotic phrasing
- overusing "cinematic"
- overusing "system"
- overusing "trust / clarity / action"
- fake metrics, fake testimonials, or fake guarantees
- making DOMINASE sound larger than it is

## 6. Work And Case Studies Direction

Do not rewrite all case studies in the first implementation pass. First, standardize the case-study structure so every project can become more credible without inventing outcomes.

Recommended structure:

1. Client context: what kind of business or experience is this?
2. What was messy before: what was scattered, manual, unclear, slow, or hard to explain?
3. What DOMINASE built: the actual website, dashboard, booking path, form flow, or interface layer.
4. What became easier: what can the visitor, customer, client team, or admin now do with less confusion?
5. What is proven: what screenshots, flows, UI states, or visible decisions can the reader inspect?
6. What is inferred: qualitative improvements that are reasonable but not measured.
7. Visual / UX decisions: hierarchy, language, mobile behavior, forms, operational screens, and trust cues.
8. Honest outcome: no metrics unless confirmed; describe the clearer experience.

English:
